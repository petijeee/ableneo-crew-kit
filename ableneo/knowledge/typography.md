# Typography

> Source of truth: **ABLENEO Design Guidelines 2025**.

## Typefaces

| Role | Typeface | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | **Ableneo** (modern geometric sans serif) | Logo, marketing, large-scale typography, anywhere brand presence is required. Weights Thin → Bold. |
| **Secondary / Web** | **Poppins** | Web, product interfaces, internal communication, and any context where Ableneo is unavailable or unsuitable. Weights Thin → Black. |

Ableneo weights: Thin · UltraLight · Light · Regular · Medium · Semibold · Bold.
Poppins weights: Thin · ExtraLight · Light · Regular · Medium · Semibold · Bold · ExtraBold · Black.

## Hierarchy (reference spec)

| Level | Font | Size | Tracking | Line height |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Headline** | Ableneo **Bold** | 48 pt | 0 | 130% (62 pt) |
| **Subheadline** | Ableneo **SemiBold** | 32 pt | 0 | 135% (43 pt) |
| **Body** | Ableneo **Regular** | 16 pt | 0 | 135% (23 pt) |

Proportional rules (scale these to any format):
- Subheadline ≈ **⅓ smaller** than the headline.
- Body ≈ **½** of the headline.
- **Highlighting** within body text: switch to **Ableneo Bold** for the words that carry the number or the point.

## Alignment
- **Center**, short text blocks, headlines, key statements, small formats where symmetry boosts impact.
- **Left**, body text and longer paragraphs; natural reading flow; multi-column / multi-section layouts.

## How this meets the voice
Typography carries the same discipline as the words (`voice-and-tone.md`):
- One headline, printable on a glass wall → that's a **Bold 48pt** line.
- Numbers over adjectives → **bold the number**, keep the rest Regular.
- Calm and precise → generous line height (130-135%), tracking at 0, no condensed cramming.
