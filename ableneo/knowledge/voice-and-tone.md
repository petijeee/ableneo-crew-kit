# Voice & Tone

> The voice is the brand. If the words aren't precise, clear, confident, and human,
> it isn't Ableneo, no matter what it says.

**Tone in four words:** Calm. Confident. Precise. Human.

## The four principles

### 1. Speak precisely, numbers over adjectives
Replace abstraction with measurable fact.

| Don't say | Say |
|---|---|
| "AI at scale" | "50+ automated processes across 3 facilities in 90 days" |
| "transformative intelligence" | "−37% invoice processing time. −22% errors in quality control" |

### 2. Speak clearly
Avoid hype, acronyms, and filler words. Short declarative sentences. One idea per line.

### 3. Speak confidently
No "maybe." No "in the future." We deliver now, not someday.

### 4. Speak humanly
No corporate theater. Write like a precise, trusted expert talking to a peer.

## The glass-wall test
> Every headline should be printable on a glass wall.

If a sentence wouldn't earn its place on a wall in the office, too long, too vague,
too hyped, cut or rewrite it.

## Verbal codes (reference register)
Short declarative sentences. Real numbers. Examples of on-brand lines:

- "We make AI clear."
- "Intelligence, implemented."
- "From chaos to clarity."
- "Built on logic. Powered by people."
- "We turn models into systems. We turn systems into performance. We turn performance into proof."

## Rhetorical patterns we use
- **Triads that escalate:** *models, systems, performance, proof.*
- **Single-sentence contrast through specificity:** name the better path concretely inside one sentence. *"We architect organizations, not the isolated processes inside them."*
- **Now, not someday:** name the timeframe and make it present.
- **Contrast pairs:** human context **and** machine precision; clarity **becomes** performance.

## Words & moves to avoid
- Hype: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "next-gen," "transformative" (unbacked).
- Hedging: "maybe," "could," "in the future," "we believe we can."
- Empty thought-leadership and vendor-speak.
- Black-box framing, we are accountable and observable, always.
- **Em dashes.** Use a comma, a period, or restructure the sentence.
- **The two-sentence reject-then-replace move** ("We don't X. We do Y."). Carry any contrast inside one sentence through specifics, never announce it across two.

## Before / after
- ❌ "Our cutting-edge AI platform could potentially help transform your operations."
- ✅ "We cut invoice processing time by 37%. In 90 days. In production."

- ❌ "Leveraging synergies between human and artificial intelligence at scale."
- ✅ "People and AI, working as one system. Measured. Governed. Proven."
