# Audience

## Who we talk to

| Tier | Who | Why they matter |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | CIOs, CTOs, COOs, Heads of Operations | They need performance they can **measure**. They sign off and own the outcome. |
| **Secondary** | Middle managers | They must **implement** the transformation day to day. |
| **Tertiary** | Employees | Their **trust** determines whether AI actually succeeds. |

## Their pain
AI hype, unclear ROI, loss of control, fatigue from false promises.

## Their need
Clarity, measurability, governance, trusted experts.

## What this means for our messaging
- **To the primary tier:** lead with measurable outcomes and ROI. Numbers, timeframes, production, not potential. Governance and accountability are features, not footnotes.
- **To the secondary tier:** show the operating model, how it runs, who owns what, how it fits real workflows.
- **To the tertiary tier:** make it understandable and trustworthy. AI as an ally that amplifies people, not replaces them.

## Category entry points
We enter the conversation when:

- The board demands ROI from AI.
- The CIO needs measurable wins.
- Operations face inefficiency fatigue.
- Teams lose trust in AI vendors.

> Map every campaign, deck, and pitch to one of these entry points. If it doesn't
> answer one of them, it's not aimed at a real moment of need.
